# Mailchimp Account Recovery Email Template

## Before You Send

**Gather this information:**
- Your current email address (the one you're using now)
- The email address associated with the Mailchimp account (the old one)
- Your billing email (if different)
- Any account verification documents (credit card statements, etc.)
- The date you created the account (approximate is fine)

---

## Email Template

**Send this to:** support@mailchimp.com

**Subject:** Account Recovery Request - Locked Out of Account (Email: [old-email@example.com])

---

**Body:**

```
Hello Mailchimp Support,

I'm writing to request help recovering access to my Mailchimp account 
that I can no longer access.

ACCOUNT DETAILS:
- Associated Email: [your-old-email@example.com]
- Account Created: [approximate date, e.g., "2023"]
- Billing Email: [if different from above]
- Current Email: [your-new-email@example.com]

SITUATION:
I created a Mailchimp account using an email address that I no longer 
have access to. I haven't used the account in [X months/years], but I 
see recurring charges of $20/month on my credit card statement.

I would like to either:
1. Update the email address associated with the account to my current 
   email ([your-new-email@example.com]), OR
2. Cancel the account and stop the recurring charges

VERIFICATION:
To verify I own this account, I can provide:
- The credit card used for billing (last 4 digits: XXXX)
- Billing statements showing the charges
- The approximate date the account was created
- Any other information you need to verify ownership

I've tried using the "Forgot Password" feature, but since I don't have 
access to the old email address, I cannot complete the recovery process.

Please help me regain access to this account or cancel it so I can stop 
the recurring charges.

Thank you for your assistance.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Phone Number]
[Your Current Email]
```

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### **Step 1: Gather Your Information**
- [ ] Old email address
- [ ] Current email address
- [ ] Approximate account creation date
- [ ] Last 4 digits of billing credit card
- [ ] Recent billing statement (screenshot or PDF)

### **Step 2: Customize the Email**
- Replace `[your-old-email@example.com]` with your actual old email
- Replace `[your-new-email@example.com]` with your current email
- Replace `[X months/years]` with how long it's been
- Replace `[Your Name]` and `[Your Phone Number]` with your info

### **Step 3: Send the Email**
- Go to **Gmail, Outlook, or your email provider**
- Compose a new email
- Send to: **support@mailchimp.com**
- Copy and paste the customized template
- Attach any verification documents (billing statements, etc.)
- Send!

### **Step 4: Wait for Response**
- Mailchimp usually responds within **24-48 hours**
- Check your email (including spam folder) for their response
- They may ask for additional verification
- Respond promptly to speed up the process

### **Step 5: Resolve the Account**
Once they respond, they'll either:
- **Update your email** ‚Üí You can log in and cancel if you want
- **Cancel the account** ‚Üí Charges stop immediately
- **Request more verification** ‚Üí Provide what they ask for

## Pro Tips

1. **Be polite and clear** - Support staff are more helpful when you're nice
2. **Provide all info upfront** - Speeds up the process
3. **Ask about refunds** - Mention the recurring charges you've been paying
4. **Keep records** - Screenshot the email you send and their response
5. **Follow up** - If no response in 48 hours, send a polite follow-up

## Alternative: Dispute with Your Credit Card

If Mailchimp support is unresponsive:

1. **Contact your credit card company**
2. **File a dispute** for unauthorized recurring charges
3. **Explain:** Account created with old email, no longer have access, can't cancel
4. **Provide:** Screenshots of charges, proof you tried to contact Mailchimp
5. **Result:** Credit card company may reverse charges and investigate

**Timeline:** 30-60 days for credit card dispute resolution
